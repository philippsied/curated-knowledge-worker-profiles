<product_market_fit>

**Sean-Ellis Test**: "How disappointed would you be without this product?"
≥40% "very disappointed" = PMF. Validated across ~100 startups.

**Superhuman Method**: Segment to high-expectation customers. Roadmap 50/50:
double what fans love / fix what hesitators resist.

**PMF confirmation** (3 signals simultaneously):
1. Sean-Ellis ≥40% with at least 40 respondents
2. Flat retention curves (week-1 is best long-term predictor)
3. >50% new accounts via organic/direct traffic

**False PMF signals:**
- Growth purely from paid acquisition
- Early-adopter enthusiasm without mainstream transfer
- High NPS with declining usage
- NRR 100% that masks 20% churn with 20% expansion
</product_market_fit>

<onboarding_framework>

**Core facts:**
- 8/10 users leave apps due to lack of understanding
- No activation within 3 days = 90% churn probability
- TTV halving can double long-term retention
- Each additional minute of TTV reduces conversion by 3%

**3-Phase Framework:**
1. **0-60 seconds**: Orientation (welcome, role selection, expectations)
2. **1-5 minutes**: Activation (guided walkthrough to first value moment)
3. **5min-7 days**: Reinforcement (checklists, tooltips, email sequences)

**Benchmarks:**
- 3-step tours: 72% completion (vs. 16% for 7-step)
- Personalized flows: +30% 7-day retention
- Top quartile: 2.3x higher activation than median
</onboarding_framework>

<product_led_growth>

**Core patterns:**
- PQLs convert at 25-30% (5-6x MQL rate)
- PLG = 15-20% higher NRR than pure sales-driven
- Future = hybrid: PLG for acquisition, sales for enterprise
- Community as multiplier: integrated ecosystems → 40% better retention

**Reference examples:** Canva (product-led SEO), Notion (100M+ users), Figma (>1B revenue)
</product_led_growth>

<customer_success>

**NRR is the most powerful growth lever:**
- NRR >100% = organic growth without new-customer dependency
- NRR >120% = 2-3x higher valuations
- At >50M ARR, expansion exceeds new-customer revenue

**Retention strategies:**
- AI churn scoring: 10-15% reduction over 18 months
- Proactive health scores: 6-12 point NRR lift
- Involuntary churn recovery: 70% of lost revenue recovered
- Upgrade trigger at 80% of tier limits
- 5% churn reduction can double growth rate
</customer_success>

<anti_patterns>

**5 deadliest patterns:**
1. **Premature Scaling**: Validate business model first. Founder-led sales must work before VP Sales.
2. **Feature Bloat**: 40-60% trial users use product exactly once. Progressive disclosure + RICE + saying no.
3. **Leaky Bucket Syndrome**: Retention curve falling → stop all growth spending, repair core value loop.
4. **Wrong Pricing**: Regular pricing optimization yields 30% higher growth rates.
5. **Organizational Errors**: Too generic market, no differentiation. Vertical + AI-native perform significantly better.
</anti_patterns>

<operational_frameworks>

**AARRR (Pirate Metrics):**
Acquisition → Activation → Retention → Revenue → Referral
For established products: **RARRA** (retention first)

**T2D3 Growth Path:**
At ~2M ARR: Triple → Triple → Double → Double → Double = 144M ARR in 5 years

**North Star Metric:**
Must: drive revenue + reflect customer value + measure progress
Examples: Slack = "messages/DAU", HubSpot = "new contacts", Dropbox = "uploads in first hour"

**Pulse check cadence:**
- Weekly: DAU/WAU, signups, activation, feature usage, support tickets, churn signals
- Monthly: MRR composition, NRR, GRR, cohorts, NPS, LTV:CAC, trial conversion
- Quarterly: Rule of 40, magic number, Sean-Ellis survey, pricing review
</operational_frameworks>
